From p-values to posteriors

Five ways to look at the same experiment

An interactive primer in five short steps. You set the hidden truth, draw samples, and watch the statistical machinery respond — first the classical t‑test, then what its guarantees do and don't promise, then the same mathematics read the Bayesian way round, then the prior made explicit, and finally the posterior turned into a decision.

STEP 1

How a classical test works

Populations, samples, the t statistic, its null distribution, and what a p‑value actually measures. Drag the truth around and watch the verdict flip.

 STEP 2

What p-values and power can't tell you

Run the machine a thousand times. Sensitivity and specificity belong to the test — but whether your positive result is real depends on the base rate.

 STEP 3

The Bayesian t-test

The same t‑distribution, re-centred on the data: from is there an effect? to how big is it, and how sure are we?

 STEP 4

The role of the prior

Prior × likelihood ∝ posterior, drawn over the whole (Δ, σ²) plane — and why a very lax prior is well-intentioned but rarely wise.

 STEP 5

The Bayesian decision

A posterior is not yet a choice. Add a reward, act on expected utility — and see why reading a confidence interval as “uniform over plausible values” decides worse.

Self-contained pages — plain HTML, SVG and vanilla JavaScript. Best read in order, sliders in hand.
