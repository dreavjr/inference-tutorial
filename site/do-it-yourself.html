Bayesian yes-no decisions with binary data

You've reached the end of the guided tour. This last stop is hands-on: a Jupyter notebook that rebuilds the whole Bayesian pipeline — prior, likelihood, posterior, and the quantity you actually decide on — for a binary classifier whose success rate you don't know.

The plumbing and the plots are written for you; you fill in the few lines at the heart of the inference, marked “⚠️ Do it yourself”.

Run it on your computer

Open it in your own Jupyter. Needs Python with numpy, scipy and matplotlib (the repo's requirements.txt has the full list). Best if you already have a local setup.
